Most people think LinkedIn sells networking. It doesn't, not exactly — the network itself, your connections and their connections, was always free to build.

LinkedIn's real product isn't the network. It's how far your visibility reaches beyond it. Everything else supports that objective.

## The Invisible Business

Imagine a recruiter searching for someone with your exact skills. Your profile matches perfectly. They still can't message you directly, and you can't see that they looked.

That ceiling isn't accidental. Behind it sits one invisible question: how much of your visibility do we hand out for free before someone needs to pay for the rest? That's the business.

Connection limits on search results. A blurred list of who viewed your profile. InMail credits that let you reach a stranger outside your network. Every layer points toward a single outcome: visibility, rationed and sold in tiers.

## Why The Network Isn't The Product

LinkedIn has hundreds of millions of free profiles. Most people assume the size of the network is the business.

The network's size is actually table stakes — it's what makes the visibility worth paying for in the first place. The real business is deciding exactly how much of that network can see you, search for you, or message you before you hit a paywall.

Viewed that way, every LinkedIn decision suddenly makes sense: why Premium never adds new connections, only new reach; why recruiters and salespeople pay the highest tiers of all; why "who viewed your profile" was one of the first features ever put behind a paywall.
