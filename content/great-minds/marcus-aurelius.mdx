Every night, in a tent on the frontier or a palace in Rome, the most powerful man alive sat down and wrote to himself. Not for readers — *Meditations* was never titled or published in his lifetime. It was a private ledger of the same argument, made again and again, in his own hand, to no audience but himself: you don't control what happens to you, only what you decide it means.

That distinction was the whole practice. He commanded legions, wrote laws, and held an empire together through plague and two decades of war — and none of that was the thing he actually worked on. The thing he worked on was smaller and harder: the part of him that reacted.

He had the power to do absolutely anything he wanted. He used his private notebook to practice wanting less.
