Imagine drawing a circle around your own kingdom.

You haven't met the neighboring rulers. You know nothing about their personalities.

Yet Chanakya believed you could already predict where alliances would form.

Why?

Because geography shapes incentives before character ever does.

Draw that circle, he taught, and the very next kingdom outward is your natural rival — not for anything it did, but simply for where it sits. The kingdom beyond that one is your natural ally, for the same reason in reverse.
